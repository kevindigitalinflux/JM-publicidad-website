## 5 Specific Content & Keyword Gaps

---

### Gap 1 — FAQ Section (zero coverage)

**What competitors do:**
Colillas dedicates a full FAQ block answering: "What is outdoor advertising?", "How much does it cost?", "Is design included?", "What are the key benefits?" — each answer targets a distinct long-tail query. Graphics Production bakes FAQ-style language into every service page ("What materials do you use for vehicle wraps?" etc.).

**What JM Publicidad has:**
Nothing. No FAQ anywhere on the site.

**Keywords being missed:**
- _"how long does outdoor signage take to produce"_
- _"how much does vehicle branding cost Spain"_
- _"what materials are used for large format printing"_
- _"do you offer design with print"_
- _"can you print and install signage Madrid"_

**Recommended fix:**
Add an FAQ section to `/services` (and optionally `/contact`) covering pricing signals, turnaround, design-included questions, and materials. Each answer is an independent long-tail ranking opportunity and feeds Google's "People Also Ask" boxes.

---

### Gap 2 — Graphic Installation & Delivery Services

**What competitors do:**
Graphics Production lists "Graphic Installation Services" as a standalone service, explicitly covering on-site installation for retail, events, and vehicle fleets. Gigantia's entire value proposition is "integrated projects — from production to installation." Both target the keyword cluster around **installation** not just production.

**What JM Publicidad has:**
Mentions "Print Media Production" but says nothing about whether they install signs, deliver wrapped vehicles, or handle logistics. Studio Specs says "10–14 Business Days" but gives no context about what that covers.

**Keywords being missed:**
- _"signage installation Madrid"_
- _"vehicle wrap installation Spain"_
- _"print and install outdoor signage"_
- _"turnkey signage company Spain"_

**Recommended fix:**
Add a "Production to Installation" subsection on the Services page. Even one paragraph explaining that JM handles end-to-end (design → print → install) is a conversion and ranking signal. This is a high-purchase-intent keyword cluster.

---

### Gap 3 — Event, Exhibition & Trade Show Graphics

**What competitors do:**
Graphics Production explicitly lists "Event graphics & trade show displays," "Exhibition graphics," "Museum displays," and "POP displays" — each as individually crawlable content. These serve a distinct buyer persona (event marketers, exhibition stand builders) who search separately from traditional signage buyers.

**What JM Publicidad has:**
Zero content. The word "event" does not appear anywhere on the site.

**Keywords being missed:**
- _"trade show display printing Spain"_
- _"exhibition stand graphics Madrid"_
- _"event banner printing Spain"_
- _"pop display production Madrid"_
- _"congress and fair printed materials Spain"_

**Recommended fix:**
Add "Event & Exhibition Graphics" as a fifth service card on `/services`. This is an existing capability for most print studios and captures a separate high-intent buyer segment entirely absent from current content.

---

### Gap 4 — Turnaround Time & Rush Production Content

**What competitors do:**
Gigantia leads with "24 hours a day" operations and explicitly promises "delivered on time" as a headline trust signal. Graphics Production uses "on-time and on-budget" as a core brand promise with dedicated project management language around timelines. Both treat turnaround time as a **primary** conversion argument, not a footnote.

**What JM Publicidad has:**
"10–14 Business Days" buried in the Studio Specs table on `/services`. No mention of rush jobs, express production, or what factors affect lead time.

**Keywords being missed:**
- _"rush signage printing Madrid"_
- _"express vehicle wrap Spain"_
- _"same week banner printing Spain"_
- _"fast turnaround large format print Spain"_

**Recommended fix:**
Surface turnaround time more prominently — ideally as a named section on `/services` with tiers (standard 10–14 days / express 5–7 days / rush on request). This doubles as a trust signal and a keyword target for urgency-driven searches, which have very high conversion rates.

---

### Gap 5 — Geographic Service Area & Local Landing Pages

**What competitors do:**
Graphics Production has separate indexed pages for `/large-format-print-signage-services-spain/` AND `/large-format-print-signage-services-madrid/` AND `/large-format-printing-madrid/` — three individually ranking pages for geo-variants of the same service. Gigantia lists offices in Madrid, Andalucía, Levante, and Portugal with region-specific content. Colillas shows distinct Barcelona and Madrid addresses with local phone numbers on every page.

**What JM Publicidad has:**
One contact address (Madrid Design District). No explicit content establishing geographic coverage. The word "Madrid" appears once, in an address string. "Spain" appears in meta tags but not in any body copy.

**Keywords being missed:**
- _"advertising agency Madrid"_
- _"signage company Madrid"_
- _"print studio Barcelona"_ (if they serve Barcelona)
- _"rotulación Madrid"_ (Spanish-language local search)
- _"publicidad exterior Madrid"_ (Spanish OOH search)

**Recommended fix:**
At minimum: weave "Madrid" and "Spain" naturally into homepage body copy and service descriptions. Stretch goal: create a dedicated `/madrid` or `/servicios` landing page in Spanish targeting `rotulación Madrid`, `publicidad exterior Madrid`, and `impresión gran formato Madrid` — the Spanish-language equivalents that competitors are not fully targeting in English.
